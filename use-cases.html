info Clinical Context

Cardiovascular diseases are the leading cause of death worldwide. Individual hospitals have limited datasets that do not represent complete population diversity, limiting the effectiveness of predictive models.

architecture Model Architecture

Input Layer: 25 clinical variables (ECG, blood tests, medical history)
Hidden Layers: 3 dense layers (128, 64, 32 neurons) with ReLU activation
Output Layer: 5-year cardiovascular risk probability (Sigmoid)

Neural network design for cardiovascular risk prediction

timeline Implementation Process

1

Project Configuration

University Hospital A acts as leader, creating the "CardioRisk Consortium" project and defining the base model.

2

Federated Connections

Each hospital configures its secure connection with automatic connectivity validation and metadata synchronization.

3

Collaborative Training

25 rounds of federated training with real-time monitoring of convergence and contribution from each hospital.

psychology Machine Learning Model

Algorithm: Federated Random Forest

  • Input variables: 15 predictors (glucose, HbA1c, BMI, family history)
  • Target variable: Probability of developing diabetes in 2 years
  • Configuration: 100 estimators, maximum depth 10
  • Validation: Federated cross-validation with 10 rounds
